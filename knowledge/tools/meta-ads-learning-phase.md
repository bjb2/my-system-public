# Meta Ads: Learning Phase & Creative Rotation Incompatibility

See also: [[domains/paid-media-incrementality]] — Meta optimizes for attributed conversions, not incremental ones; learning phase exit does not mean the campaign is genuinely incremental.

## The math

Meta needs ~50 conversion events per ad to exit learning. At $10 CPA = $500/ad.

At typical testing budgets ($10-20/day per ad set split across 2-3 ads), each ad gets $5-10/day = **50-100 days to exit learning.**

If a creative has a shelf life shorter than that window (event-specific ads, seasonal content, anything time-sensitive), **it will never exit learning before it's deprecated.** You permanently pay learning-phase CPAs.

## Implication for event-based creatives

3-events format showing specific named events goes stale in 1-2 weeks. Learning window at low budget = 50+ days. Structurally incompatible.

A "winning" ad in this format may just be the one that's been running longest — not genuinely better creative.

## The fix: evergreen formats compound, time-sensitive formats don't

Formats that accumulate signal over time:
- **Identity/emotion angle** ("Tired of ending up at the same 3 places?") — no events, no dates, runs indefinitely
- **Near Miss** ("Last Thursday, 40 people showed up to...") — past tense intentional, evergreen
- **Benefit/product** — describes the subscription value, not specific events

Formats that reset the clock on every rotation:
- Specific named events with dates
- Seasonal/timely content
- Anything requiring weekly swap

## Strategic rule

For small budgets (<$50/day per ad): **evergreen creative is mandatory as the foundation.** Time-sensitive creative can supplement but cannot be the core. Otherwise you're always in learning phase.

Higher budgets can exit learning faster (50 conversions in 10 days at $50/day + $10 CPA), making rotation viable.
